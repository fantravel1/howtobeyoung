About HowToBeYoung

The global women's longevity playbook. Science-based. Culture-respecting. Not rich-person wellness. Not diet culture. Not cosmetic. Free.

Our Mission

Filling the Biggest Gaps in Online Health Content for Women

We exist to fill the biggest gaps in online health content for women. The internet is full of longevity advice -- but almost none of it is built for the women who need it most.

1. Women's Longevity That Isn't Fertility-Centered

The vast majority of women's health content online revolves around fertility, pregnancy, and reproductive outcomes. But women's longevity is about far more than the ability to have children. It is about cardiovascular health, brain resilience, muscle preservation, hormonal balance across all life stages, and decades of functional independence. We center the whole woman -- not just her reproductive system.

2. Youth Preservation That Isn't Rich-People Wellness

Most "stay young" content assumes you have access to a functional medicine doctor, a $400 monthly supplement budget, a personal trainer, and a Whole Foods within walking distance. That is not the real world. Our protocols are designed for women on normal budgets, in normal cities, with normal jobs. If it requires a biohacking lab or a concierge physician, it does not belong here.

3. Non-Western Longevity Approaches Treated with Respect

Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine, African herbal traditions, Andean healing practices, and Mediterranean folk wisdom have sustained human health for centuries. Western wellness culture often either ignores these traditions or appropriates them without context. We treat non-Western longevity approaches as legitimate knowledge systems -- validated by their own evidence bases and increasingly supported by modern research.

4. Youth as Something Measurable Without Medical Gatekeeping

Biological age testing is typically locked behind expensive labs and specialist appointments. But many of the most important youth markers -- resting heart rate, sleep quality, grip strength, mobility range, cognitive sharpness, stress recovery -- can be tracked at home with no equipment and no doctor's visit. We believe every woman should be able to measure her own youth without asking permission from the medical system.

5. Aging Prevention That Isn't Cosmetic

Search "how to stay young" online, and you will find pages of skincare routines, Botox guides, and cosmetic procedures. There is nothing wrong with skincare. But looking young and being young are fundamentally different things. We focus on the biological systems that determine how your body actually ages -- cardiovascular fitness, muscle mass, brain plasticity, immune function, hormonal balance -- not how your face looks in a photograph.

6. Longevity Content That Doesn't Trigger Disordered Eating

A staggering amount of "anti-aging" and "longevity" content is just diet culture with a science veneer. Caloric restriction obsession, intermittent fasting dogma, elimination diets presented as longevity hacks -- these can be deeply harmful, especially for women with histories of disordered eating. We write about nutrition for longevity without centering restriction, thinness, or shame. Every food-related article on this site includes safety notes for readers with eating disorder histories.

Content Standard

Every Article Meets Five Criteria

We hold every piece of content on this site to a strict five-point standard. If an article does not meet all five criteria, it does not go live.

1

What to Do

Clear, specific, actionable instructions. No vague wellness platitudes. You will know exactly what to do, in what order, and for how long.

2

Why It Works (Science)

The biological mechanism explained in plain language, backed by peer-reviewed research. You deserve to understand the science, not just follow orders.

3

How to Do It If You're Broke

A low-budget or no-budget adaptation of every recommendation. If the science only works with expensive tools, we find the free alternative or we don't publish it.

4

How to Adapt in Different Cultures

Cultural context for food availability, social norms, traditional practices, and regional variations. A protocol that only works in North America is not a global protocol.

5

How to Track If It's Working

Measurable indicators you can monitor at home -- no expensive lab work required. If you can't measure it, you can't improve it, and we won't ask you to take it on faith.

Our Promise

If an article doesn't meet all five criteria, it doesn't go live. No exceptions. No "we'll add that later." Every page you read on this site has earned its place.

Ethical Standards

How We Write and What We Will Never Do

We do not:

  • ✗ Promote shame about aging, body size, or appearance as motivation
  • ✗ Sell miracle cures, unproven supplements, or "secret" longevity hacks
  • ✗ Center thinness as a proxy for health or youth
  • ✗ Exploit or decontextualize cultural practices for aesthetic content

We do:

  • ✓ Center function over aesthetics -- what your body can do matters more than how it looks
  • ✓ Write clearly, directly, and respectfully -- no jargon walls, no condescension
  • ✓ Provide low-access adaptations for every recommendation we make
  • ✓ Include safety notes wherever content could affect vulnerable readers

Eating Disorder Safety

A lot of "stay young" content is essentially diet culture, fear of fat, shame, and restriction obsession. We teach youth and health without becoming eating-disorder fuel. Every nutrition article on this site is reviewed for language that could trigger disordered eating patterns. We do not celebrate caloric restriction as a longevity tool. We do not frame fasting as moral discipline. We do not use before-and-after photos that center weight loss. If you have a history of disordered eating, this site is designed to be safe for you.
