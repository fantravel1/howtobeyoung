How We Calculate Your Score

The Youth Score evaluates 8 interconnected systems that longevity research has identified as the most reliable markers of biological aging. Each system is self-rated on a 0–10 scale, then averaged and normalized to a 0–100 score.

The 8 Systems Explained

Resting Heart Rate — A lower resting heart rate generally indicates better cardiovascular fitness and more efficient heart function. Athletes and highly fit individuals typically range from 40–60 bpm, while sedentary individuals may be 80+ bpm.

Sleep Quality — Deep, restorative sleep is when your body repairs cellular damage, consolidates memory, and regulates hormones. Chronic poor sleep accelerates every marker of biological aging.

Strength Baseline — Grip strength and the ability to perform functional movements (push-ups, squats, carries) are among the strongest predictors of all-cause mortality and healthspan.

Mobility — Full range of motion without pain reflects healthy joints, fascia, and nervous system coordination. Loss of mobility is one of the earliest signs of accelerated aging.

Inflammation Markers — Chronic low-grade inflammation ("inflammaging") drives nearly every age-related disease. Joint pain, bloating, fatigue, and skin issues are accessible self-reported indicators.

Mental Sharpness — Cognitive processing speed, working memory, and focus reflect brain health. Decline in these areas can precede physical aging markers by years.

Stress Resilience — Your ability to recover from stressful events reflects autonomic nervous system health, cortisol regulation, and psychological flexibility — all core longevity factors.

Cycle Health — For those who menstruate, cycle regularity and symptom severity reflect hormonal balance, thyroid function, and overall metabolic health — key biological youth indicators.

Important Note

This is not a medical diagnosis. It is a self-awareness tool based on longevity research. Use it to track trends over time and to identify which systems deserve your attention. Always consult a healthcare professional for medical concerns.
